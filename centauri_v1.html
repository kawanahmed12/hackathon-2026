FAQs
Common questions
Who is Centauri for? ▾
Centauri is for young people aged 16–30 in the UK who lack access to the devices, connectivity, networks, or mentorship they need to progress in education or employment in tech.
Is it free to use? ▾
Yes — completely free. Centauri is a community platform supported by partner organisations and donations. There is no cost to find a device, access learning resources, or connect with a mentor.
How does the AI matching work? ▾
You fill in a short form with your location, age, and needs. Our AI analyses that information alongside a database of partner organisations, available devices, and available mentors near you, then generates a personalised list of matches with an explanation for each.
What is a Centauri mentor? ▾
Centauri mentors are vetted tech professionals from Birmingham City University's Centauri network. They offer dedicated one-to-one guidance to young people who have no existing connections in the industry — from career planning to project reviews and introductions.
Can I donate on behalf of my company? ▾
Absolutely. Business donations are welcome and may qualify for tax relief. Head to the Donate Tech page to register devices and request a donation certificate.

    No network, no foot in the door

    70% of jobs are never publicly advertised. Without industry contacts, young people from underserved backgrounds are locked out from the start.

    No one to ask for advice

    Career guidance at school rarely covers tech careers in depth. Without a mentor, young people navigate an unfamiliar industry entirely alone.

    Self-taught developers, designers, and analysts often build projects with no professional feedback — making it harder to stand out to employers.

    Geography is a barrier

    Tech hubs are concentrated in London. Young people in Birmingham and other cities are less likely to encounter working professionals in tech day to day.
